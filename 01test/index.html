Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the ability to navigate digital environments safely, responsibly, and respectfully.

What is Digital Law?

In the context of digital citizenship, Digital Law refers to the legal rights, restrictions, and electronic responsibilities governing technology use. It defines the rules and policies that dictate what users are and are not allowed to do online, ensuring that digital actions are ethical and abide by the laws of society.

What is AUP?

In the context of digital citizenship, an AUP (Acceptable Use Policy) is a formal agreement that outlines the rules and guidelines for using an organization's technology resources, such as its network, devices, and the internet.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of presenting someone else's work, ideas, or language as your own without proper credit.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is any action that undermines the integrity of the educational process by using deceptive or unauthorized means to gain an unfair advantage.

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

In 2026, the rules for AI-generated content (AIGC) have shifted from voluntary guidelines to mandatory legal and academic requirements. These rules focus on transparency, accountability, and the protection of intellectual property.
