NBA HEADLINES

The NBA landscape shifted significantly on Sunday with the official announcement of the 2026 All-Star reserves. LeBron James continues to rewrite history, earning a record-extending 22nd consecutive All-Star berth. Joining him in the headlines are first-time selections Jamal Murray of the Nuggets and Deni Avdija of the Trail Blazers, the latter becoming the first Israeli player to receive the honor. In a notable snub, the host-city LA Clippers failed to land a single player on the roster, sparking criticism from head coach Tyronn Lue.

On the court, Jarrett Allen delivered one of the most efficient performances in league history during the Cavaliers' recent victory over Portland. Allen recorded a career-high 40 points, 17 rebounds, and 4 blocks, making him the first player to ever post a 40/15/5 stat line in under 30 minutes of play. Meanwhile, Shai Gilgeous-Alexander led the Oklahoma City Thunder to a dominant win over Denver, extending his unprecedented streak of 120 consecutive games with at least 20 points.

The lead-up to the February 5 trade deadline has reached a fever pitch. A significant three-team trade was finalized between the Cavaliers, Kings, and Bulls, resulting in Cleveland acquiring veteran guard Dennis Schröder and Keon Ellis. Speculation also continues to mount regarding Giannis Antetokounmpo; while no deal is imminent, the Warriors, Knicks, and Heat have emerged as the primary frontrunners should the Bucks decide to pivot toward a rebuild before Thursday.

The league also handed out one of its most severe penalties of the season, suspending Philadelphia 76ers forward Paul George for 25 games for a violation of the NBA/NBPA Anti-Drug Program. This comes as a massive blow to the Sixers, who are already managing Joel Embiid’s right ankle injury. In the standings, the Detroit Pistons (36-12) and Oklahoma City Thunder (39-11) maintain their holds on the top seeds in their respective conferences as the league enters the final stretch before the All-Star break.

Other News

NBA Commissioner Adam Silver recently confirmed that a definitive decision regarding domestic expansion is expected later this year in 2026. Las Vegas and Seattle remain the clear frontrunners for new franchises. Silver noted that the league is currently conducting a deep economic analysis of these markets, particularly focusing on arena infrastructure and the impact of diluting current team equity from 30 to 32 owners.

In the NBA G-League, the Iowa Wolves secured a thrilling 127–123 overtime victory against the Santa Cruz Warriors this weekend, led by an Alize Johnson triple-double. On the officiating front, the NBA's new "Heave Rule" is in full effect this season; players can now attempt shots from 36 feet or further in the final three seconds of the first three quarters without it affecting their individual shooting percentages. Additionally, the Replay Center has officially taken over the primary role in reviewing out-of-bounds coach's challenges to expedite game flow.
